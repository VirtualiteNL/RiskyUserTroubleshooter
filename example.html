Overall Risk Assessment
The risk profile suggests a low to medium risk level, primarily driven by session anomalies rather than user configuration. The presence of "Suspicious inbox rules" indicates potential unauthorized access or manipulation of the user's email settings, which is a concern for data exfiltration or phishing. The sign-in logs reveal multiple instances of impossible travel, indicating either the use of VPNs/proxies or unauthorized access from various locations. However, the low SignInRisk scores and the absence of high-risk factors such as MFA failures or high abuse scores suggest that these anomalies might not indicate active exploitation. The Conditional Access policy CA001-Global-IdentityProtection-AnyApp-AnyPlatform-MFA was enabled after these sign-ins, which could mitigate similar risks moving forward.
Attack Profile Summary
The attack profile suggests potential reconnaissance or initial access attempts rather than advanced persistence or lateral movement. The "Suspicious inbox rules" could indicate an attacker's attempt to maintain access to incoming communications, possibly to monitor for specific keywords or to hide malicious activities. The impossible travel detected across sign-ins suggests either the use of anonymizing services by the user or unauthorized access attempts from various geographic locations. However, the consistent low risk levels and the absence of high abuse scores or MFA failures do not strongly indicate a sophisticated or persistent threat actor at work. The enabled Conditional Access policy for MFA across all apps and platforms should help in mitigating the risk of unauthorized access attempts in the future.
Conditional Access Policy Evaluation
The CA001-Global-IdentityProtection-AnyApp-AnyPlatform-MFA policy, which requires MFA for all apps and platforms for all users, was enabled after the risky sign-ins occurred. This risk has since been mitigated by current CA policy enforcement. No further CA policy suggestions are necessary based on the observed user behavior and the existing CA policy coverage.
